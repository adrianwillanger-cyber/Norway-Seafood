How this dashboard is built

Every number here is reconciled to a primary source before it ships. This page documents what those sources are, how monthly granularity is reconstructed where the publishers report quarterly, and what triggers an update vs. a silent end.

Primary source hierarchy

The dashboard ranks sources by proximity to first publication. NSC press releases are treated as ground truth for the US channel; trade-press articles function as cross-references that must agree with NSC within ±2% before a refresh is allowed to proceed.

Rank Source Role Cadence
1 Norwegian Seafood Council monthly & annual releases Ground truth — US-channel value, volume, % YoY, tariff commentary Monthly, ~3rd–8th of following month
2 FishFarmingExpert Cross-reference — typically quotes NSC analyst Paul T. Aandahl directly Same-day as NSC release
3 SalmonBusiness Cross-reference — destination-market breakdown (Poland, China, Spain) Same-day as NSC release
4 SeafoodNews Cross-reference — species-level color (salmon fillet, king crab, mackerel) Within 24h of NSC release
5 Norges Bank FX — NOK/USD monthly averages for the USD chart toggle Monthly

Cross-reference reconciliation (±2% rule)

Every monthly refresh extracts the same headline figures from at least two of the cross-references. If any cross-reference disagrees with NSC by more than 2% on salmon value or volume to the US, the dashboard does not update silently — the gap is logged to the changelog and surfaced in the freshness badge with an amber "gap flagged" state. This is the executable version of the "we cross-check" claim.

Monthly data classification

Two tiers of data live in the dashboard, and pros should know which they're reading.

2026 onwards — Official

Every 2026 monthly figure for salmon value, total US value, and volume is reported directly by NSC in their monthly US-channel press releases. No interpolation. These are the numbers a Norwegian Seafood Council analyst would defend.

2024–2025 — Estimated (monthly granularity)

NSC publishes monthly totals for all seafood exports, but their US-channel detail is most reliably reported quarterly during 2024–2025. To reconstruct monthly granularity for the visualisation, the dashboard applies:

monthly_value[i] = 2024_baseline[same_month] × (1 + quarterly_YoY_growth) × seasonality_factor where quarterly_YoY_growth ∈ {+55%, +20%, +6%, -1%} for Q1–Q4 2025 and seasonality_factor reflects Atlantic-salmon shipping cadence (lower in Q3, peaks in pre-Lenten Q1 and Q4 holiday).

2024 baselines are taken directly from NSC's 2025 annual report. The 2025 quarterly cadence (+55% / +20% / +6% / −1%) comes from the same source.

Pre-tariff counterfactual

The dashed line on the value chart is the answer to one question: "What would Norway have exported to the US if the April 2025 tariff regime had never happened?"

The counterfactual extends the Q1 2025 trajectory at constant +47% YoY uplift on salmon value and +30% YoY on volume — both figures published by NSC as the pre-tariff Q1 growth rate. From April 2025 forward, the line is computed against same-month-prior-year baselines × this uplift, with the same seasonality factor as the realised series. The gap between bars and line is the cumulative tariff cost, in NOK.

FX (NOK ↔ USD)

The USD currency toggle uses Norges Bank monthly average NOK/USD rates. For 2026 these are applied per month. For 2024 and 2025 the dashboard uses Norges Bank calendar-year averages (10.6 NOK/USD for 2024, 10.3 for 2025) — point-in-time monthly rates for historical months will be backfilled in a future revision; see the changelog for status.

Refresh cadence & update logic

An automated task runs daily at 7am Pacific between the 5th and 12th of each month — the window when NSC publishes its prior-month figures.

Each run does this:

  • Fetches the latest NSC news archive and identifies the most recent monthly US-channel release
  • Compares the latest month present in the release against the last month already in data.js
  • If no new month is found, writes a one-line note to the cron log and ends silently — no email, no commit, no notification
  • If new data is found, extracts salmon NOK to US, total US value, volume to US, tariff rate, and FX from the release
  • Pulls 2–3 cross-reference articles and checks each against NSC within ±2% on salmon value and volume
  • If any cross-reference fails the ±2% gate, the dashboard is not updated; the gap is logged and the freshness badge flips to "gap flagged" until manually reconciled
  • If all checks pass, data.js is updated, the site is redeployed, the signature file is bumped, and an email digest goes out

What this dashboard is not

  • Not a real-time price feed. NSC publishes monthly; the dashboard's resolution matches that. For weekly Norwegian salmon spot prices, see SSB's weekly export bulletin — that's a separate, global series.
  • Not a tariff legal-analysis tool. The tariff timeline reflects what was reported in trade press; for the underlying instrument and product-line specifics, see the USTR notices referenced in the source links.
  • Not a CMA, valuation, or investment advice. This is market intelligence for industry context — equal-housing & equal-opportunity-trade notwithstanding.

Corrections policy

If anything in here is wrong, the correction lands in the next refresh, gets a dated entry in the changelog, and the freshness badge reflects the new state. Contact: Adrian@adrianwillanger.com.
