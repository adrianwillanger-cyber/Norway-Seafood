Tariff regime timeline

Two compounding shocks reshaped the US channel in 2025

  1. Q1 2025
    0%
    Pre-tariff peak. Salmon exports to the US grew +47% YoY in Q1; total seafood value to US rose +55%. The US was on track to become Norway's largest single market.
  2. Apr 2025
    15%
    Round 1 — reciprocal tariff. Trump administration imposes a 10% baseline tariff and 15% for trade-surplus countries. Norway lands at 15%, vs. 10% for the UK — an immediate competitive disadvantage.
  3. Q2–Q3
    15%
    Channel friction. Q2 value growth decelerates to +20%; Q3 to just +6%. Importers absorb part of the levy; price per kg to the US compresses against other markets.
  4. Aug 2025
    15%+
    Round 2 — escalation. Tariffs raised again on select Norwegian seafood lines. Effective rate now 15% or higher on all products.
  5. Q4 2025
    15%+
    First negative quarter. Value to US falls −1% YoY. Strong krone and weaker dollar amplify the tariff drag.
  6. Jan 2026
    15%+
    Tariff effect crystallises. Salmon value to US drops −37%, volume −29%. US falls behind Poland, China and the Netherlands as a salmon destination.
